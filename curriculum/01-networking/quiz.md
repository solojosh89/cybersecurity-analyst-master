# Module 01 Checkpoint Quiz

Answer every question from memory, in your own words, no lookup. This is the evidence that the 18 topics claimed complete on 2026-07-19 are actually retained, not just covered. Answer in `notes.md`; whatever's shaky becomes the real "next lesson," not NAT.

1. What is an IP address, and why does a device need one to communicate on a network?
2. What is a MAC address, and how is it different from an IP address?
3. What does DHCP do, in one sentence?
4. What does ARP do, and when would a device need it?
5. What is the difference between a switch and a router?
6. What is a packet? Why is data broken into packets instead of sent as one big chunk?
7. What is a port, and why does an IP address alone not fully identify "where" data is going on a device?
8. What's the practical difference between TCP and UDP? Give one example of something that would use each.
9. What does DNS do? What would break if DNS stopped working, even if the internet itself was fine?
10. What is the difference between HTTP and HTTPS?
11. What's the difference between a GET request and a POST request?
12. Give one example status code and what it communicates (e.g. what does a 404 mean?).
13. What is a cookie, and what problem does it solve?
14. What is a session, and how does it relate to cookies?
15. What is the difference between authentication and authorization? (This one trips up even working analysts, be precise.)
16. What does a firewall actually do? What decision is it making?
17. Pick ONE connection from a `Get-NetTCPConnection`-style output (LocalAddress, LocalPort, RemoteAddress, RemotePort, State) and explain what each column means, using terms 4-11 above.
18. What is NAT, in your own words, even though it's "not yet covered" - take a guess before the real lesson, same as every Module 00 prediction.
